The Origin

From Solitary
to the Stage

At 23, Coss Marte was running one of the most profitable drug operations in New York City's Lower East Side, pulling in over $2 million a year. Then came the arrest, the conviction, and a 7-year sentence.

Inside, a prison doctor told him he could die of a heart attack within 5 years. That was the turning point. In a 9-by-6-foot cell, with no equipment and no space, Coss created a bodyweight workout system and lost 70 pounds in 6 months.

"They told me I was going to die. I decided to live."

After release, he founded ConBody — the first fitness studio in America staffed entirely by formerly incarcerated individuals. Featured on Ellen, CNN, TEDx, and Forbes, Coss has turned his second chance into a movement.
